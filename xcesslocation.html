Travelling to Pattaya

By car:

Travelers can easily reach Pattaya with just a two-hour drive along the picturesque Bangna-Trat Highway, passing through Bang Pakong, Chonburi, and Si Racha. For those seeking an alternative route, Highway 36, the newly constructed Chon Buri - Pattaya - Rayong route, offers a bypass of Chonburi, providing a smoother journey to your destination.

By bus:

From Bangkok, regular air-conditioned buses depart daily from both the Eastern Bus Terminal (Ekkamai) and the Northern Bus Terminal (Mochit). At the Eastern Bus Terminal, buses leave every half hour from 5:00 am to 10:00 pm, while at the Northern Bus Terminal, departures are frequent between 5:00 am to 7:00 pm. Another convenient option is catching a mini-van from Century One shopping mall near Victory Monument, just a short distance from the hotel. These mini-vans provide a swift and convenient ride, taking only 1.5 hours and costing Bht 120. For those seeking a direct route from Bangkok Suvarnabhumi International Airport to Pattaya, a few minibuses depart daily at 9:00 am, 12:00 pm, and 7:00 pm.

Getting Around Pattaya

Baht Buses:

In Pattaya, "Baht Buses" are distinctive blue pick-up trucks equipped with a sturdy canopy top and bench seats in the back. Getting around Pattaya is simple: just flag down a passing driver with a small gesture, hop on board, and wait until you're about five meters from your destination. Then, ring the bell located on the ceiling, and your driver will stop accordingly. Payment is made through the window on the pavement side, so it's advisable to keep some change handy as drivers prefer smaller notes. The standard fare for foreigners is typically 10 baht for any stop along the route. For longer distances, fares are clearly displayed in the back of every taxi.

Taxi:

In Pattaya, proper taxis with air-conditioning are readily available for travel to any destination, not just the airport. These taxis typically do not operate on meters, and fares are usually negotiated. However, there are standard fares for common routes, often advertised by most taxi companies. While most drivers speak some English and can provide valuable information, if you prefer a hassle-free option, our hotel can arrange transportation to any destination. Simply inquire at our Travel Counter for more details and assistance.

Moto:

In Pattaya, motorbike-taxis are an integral part of daily life. When you need to reach your destination quickly or conveniently, especially during busy periods, they are your best bet. You'll find clusters of motorbike taxis at every corner in Pattaya, operating around the clock, seven days a week. These drivers are ready to take you anywhere for a fare ranging from 20 to 50 baht, depending on the distance. As is customary in Thailand, remember to negotiate the fare before starting your journey.

Bolt:

Bolt is a convenient ride-sharing app that makes getting around easy and affordable. With Bolt, you can book a ride quickly using your phone, enjoy safe and reliable drivers, and benefit from transparent pricing. Whether you need a ride day or night, Bolt is available 24/7, offering cashless transactions and fast response times. Experience hassle-free transportation with Bolt wherever you go!

Hire Cars and Motorbikes:

Another practical way to explore Pattaya is by renting a motorbike. For around 150 baht per day, or even less for longer rentals, you can hire a scooter-type bike like the Honda Dream, known for its reliability and user-friendly features like automatic gears. With traffic congestion in the area, local traffic moves slowly, making motorbikes ideal for experienced riders to navigate through. Enjoy the flexibility and convenience of zipping around the city on your own schedule!

While cars can be hired in Pattaya, driving them in and around the city, especially during peak times or on Sukhumvit Road, can be frustrating due to traffic congestion. Generally, four-wheel vehicles are most useful during rainy weather or if you plan to explore the surrounding areas. Rental prices start at around 500 baht per day for older cars, ranging up to 1,200 baht for more modern or four-wheel-drive vehicles.
